About Us

Parque Luro Provincial Reserve, a tourist icon in the province of La Pampa, located 35 km south of the city of Santa Rosa, combines history, culture and nature. It is a protected area of ​​7,600 ha that conserves the ecosystem of the caldén forest, with a great diversity of flora and avifauna, and dune, valley and salt flat landscapes. Originally, this property was the first hunting ground in the country, called "San Huberto" (patron saint of hunters), where the first red deer and European wild boar were introduced in Argentina. The original genetics of the red deer in the wild in the Pampas forest can be seen in this place. One of the main attractions of the Reserve is an old mansion, today the "El Castillo" Museum, whose construction began in 1905. It was declared a national historical monument and currently preserves the original design and furniture of its first owners: Don Pedro Olegario Luro and Don Antonio Maura.

THE WEATHER

La Pampa has a low humidity index, its climate is temperate and it does not have a particular rainy season. The seasons in the southern hemisphere are opposite to the seasons in the northern hemisphere, with temperatures ranging from -1°C to 35°C depending on the season. Therefore, it is a good destination to escape from the cold winters or the heat of summer. In Argentina, during the summer (December, January and February) temperatures rise up to 35°C. In March, April and May it is moderate, with temperatures around 15°C and 28°C. As for clothing, comfortable and fresh clothing is recommended for the day and warmer for the morning and night. In June, July and August, the temperature drops a bit and ranges between -1°C and 20°C. Therefore, warm clothing is suggested.
